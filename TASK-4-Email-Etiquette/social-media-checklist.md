# The College Student's Social Media Checklist        

## DO’S:  
* &#x20; **Do audit your privacy settings.**    
Keep your personal accounts restricted to friends and family to protect your privacy and control who sees your casual updates.

* &#x20; **Do build a professional digital footprint.**   
Use platforms like LinkedIn or GitHub to showcase your Python and C++ projects, or highlight your involvement in campus groups like a sponsorship committee.

* &#x20; **Do verify information before sharing.**    
Double check facts, especially for news, campus announcements, or academic resources, before passing them along to peers.

* &#x20; **Do network with peers and alumni.**    
Connect with classmates, professors, and professionals in your field to build relationships that can open doors for internships or jobs later on.

* &#x20; **Do take intentional screen breaks.**    
Step away from the feeds regularly to get out on the football pitch, listen to some music, or just recharge offline.


## DON’TS:
* &#x20; **Don't overshare sensitive information.** 
Avoid posting your real-time location, dorm room number, or photos of your student ID card.

* &#x20; **Don't engage in digital drama.**  
Avoid getting pulled into heated arguments in comment sections, and never participate in cyberbullying.

* &#x20; **Don't let scrolling derail your focus.**  
Set app timers so social media doesn't eat up the time you need to prep for demanding exams like vector calculus or AI/ML.

* &#x20; **Don't post anything you wouldn't want an employer to see.**  
Unprofessional photos, illegal activities, or aggressive rants can easily come back to haunt you during background checks.

* &#x20; **Don't compare your everyday life to a highlight reel.**   
Remember that social media is highly curated; don't let someone else's filtered posts impact your mental health or confidence.
